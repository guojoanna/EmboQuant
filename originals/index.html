Clinical Problem

Interventional radiologists need an objective method to consistently deliver an optimal quantity of embolic agents, in order to prevent complications and repeat procedures. In the current method of endpoint determination known as "beats of stasis", the physician counts the number of heart beats for injected contrast to wash away, which is subjective and variable.
